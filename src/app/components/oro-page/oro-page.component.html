Testing Methodology

Outline of the process our curators use to rank weapons

The Orokin 100

The Orokin 100 challenge, usually shortened to oro100, requires the following setup:

Using the Citadel Simulacrum.
Warframe with 1.00 movespeed and no passive DPS increase.
Only two mods are installed: Enemy Sense and Enemy Radar.
Two of each of the following enemies (total of 16 out of maximum 20):
  • Corrupted Lancer
  • Corrupted Crewman
  • Corrupted Butcher
  • Corrupted Moa
  • Corrupted Heavy Gunner
  • Corrupted Ancient
  • Corrupted Bombard
  • Corrupted Nullifier
Friendly Fire: OFF
Invincibility: ON
Pause AI: OFF
Enemy Level: 100

To ensure enemies spawn according to a fixed pattern and minimize variations:
  1. Simulate the enemies ONCE
  2. Return to the console and kill enemies
  3. Exit the console
  4. Go to the console a third time and simulate again

The time it takes to kill all 16 enemies is considered the scored for that run.

Testing Procedure

The exact procedure used by our curators


Standard test

Tester should attempt up to three test runs to familiarize themselves with the weapon.
Minimum of five (5) complete attempts are made per weapon.

A run is considered incomplete if:
  • Tester drops out of bounds due to mistake in maneuver. If the mistake is induced by the weapon the run continues.
  • An enemy drops out of bounds before fatal damage is dealt.
  • Tester fails to reset all conditions prior to run. This commonly includes, but is not limited to: Berserker proc(s), Bladed Rounds, failing to reload and pick up ammo, sniper combo, and others.
  • Any other errors that will severely impact results.

Depending on a weapon's performance, up to 11 runs may be completed.

The following criteria is used to determine number of runs attempted:
  • 5 runs - All 5 runs fall below Top for that arsenal slot
  • 7 runs - Out of 5 runs, at least one (1) run fall within Top
  • 9 runs - Out of 7 runs, at least three (3) runs within Top
  • 11 runs - Out of 9 runs, at least five (5) runs fall within Top.

Final Scoring:

After all runs are completed, the five (5) fastest runs will be used for final scoring.
The final score is calculated with this formula:

ROUND ( [ Average of 5 fastest runs ] * ( 1 + [ Standard Deviation of 5 Fastest runs ] / 2) , 2 )

This ensures that the average time of each weapon is considered, while penalizing potential inconsistencies of the weapon.
